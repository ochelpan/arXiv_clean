Practical lower bounds for hybrid quantum interior point methods in linear programming

Authors: Lennart Binkowski
Type: theory · Category: quantum information and computing · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.24362v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. Determining whether the asymptotic polynomial speed-ups promised by hybrid quantum interior point methods (QIPMs) translate into practical advantages for solving linear programming problems on realistic instances.

Main result. The study establishes that quantum runtime lower bounds exceed classical runtimes for all tested instances at realistic quantum cycle durations, effectively ruling out practical advantage for these hybrid QIPMs.

Method. A hybrid benchmarking approach that derives rigorous lower bounds on quantum runtime using highly benevolent assumptions and compares them against the classical HiGHS solver.

Summary. This paper investigates the practical viability of hybrid quantum algorithms for linear programming. By comparing the best-case quantum runtime lower bounds against state-of-the-art classical solvers, the author demonstrates that the overhead from state tomography and the complexity of the Newton systems prevent any practical advantage. The findings suggest that for realistic problem instances and hardware cycle times, classical solvers like HiGHS remain superior.

Detailed structure

Model / system. The mathematical model is Linear Programming (LP) solved via Primal-Dual Path-Following Interior Point Methods, utilizing a quantum pipeline with Chebyshev-based Quantum Linear System Algorithms (QLSAs) and state tomography.

Key observables. Quantum runtime lower bounds, effective difficulty (product of sparsity and condition number), and tomography overhead.

Important parameters / regimes. Sparsity (s), condition number (kappa), target precision (epsilon), and quantum cycle duration.

Assumptions / limitations. Highly benevolent assumptions including noise-free hardware, single-cycle oracle execution, convergence in a single iteration, and minimal iterative refinement steps.

Figures summary. Figure 1 illustrates the quantum runtime lower bound pipeline; Figure 2 shows the distribution of effective difficulty across different problem classes; Figure 3 plots the percentage of instances where quantum lower bounds are below classical runtimes relative to quantum cycle duration.

Paper structure. The paper introduces the QIPM framework, defines the benchmarking methodology and benevolent assumptions, presents the numerical estimation of system parameters, provides the exclusion analysis results across various problem families, and identifies the tomography overhead as the primary bottleneck.

Abstract

Quantum interior point methods (QIPMs) promise polynomial speed-ups over classical solvers for linear programming by outsourcing the solution of Newton linear systems to quantum linear solvers (QLSAs). However, asymptotic speed-ups do not necessarily translate to practical advantages on realistic problem instances. In this work, I evaluate whether practical advantage of a standard hybrid QIPM pipeline can already be excluded relative to the classical open-source solver HiGHS on a broad and diverse collection of LP instances spanning eight problem families, including public benchmark libraries, such as MIPlib, and relaxations of combinatorial optimisation problems. Following the hybrid benchmarking paradigm initiated by Cade et al., I derive rigorous lower bounds on the quantum runtime under a series of highly benevolent assumptions and compare them against classical runtimes. I equip the QIPMs with the best-performing functional QLSA, the Chebyshev-based method, as identified by Lefterovici et al., and evaluate two Newton system formulations proposed by Mohammadisiahroudi et al.: the modified normal equation system and the orthogonal subspace system. The exclusion analysis yields a consistent negative picture: across all instances and for any realistic quantum cycle duration, the quantum runtime lower bounds already exceed the classical runtimes, establishing that these hybrid QIPMs will offer no practical advantage over good classical solvers for realistic linear programming instances.

Quantum Circuit Cutting: Complexity and Optimization

Authors: Yuval Idan, Eitan Zahavi, Elad Mentovich, Eliahu Cohen, Shmuel Zaks
Type: theory · Category: quantum information and computing · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23700v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. The paper addresses the computational complexity and optimization of quantum circuit cutting, which involves partitioning large quantum circuits into smaller fragments to be processed on multiple smaller QPUs.

Main result. The authors prove that finding the optimal circuit-cutting configuration (the Graph Duplication problem) is NP-complete in general and even for simplified 2-qubit gate circuits, but can be solved in polynomial time if the number of cuts is constant.

Method. The work uses a graph-theoretic framework mapping circuits to Directed A-cyclic Graphs (DAGs) and employs reductions from the 3-partition problem to establish complexity, alongside an SMT-based solver for practical optimization.

Summary. This paper provides a rigorous complexity-theoretic analysis of quantum circuit cutting, a vital technique for executing large circuits on fragmented NISQ hardware. By mapping the cutting process to a graph duplication problem, the authors demonstrate that finding optimal cuts is NP-complete. However, they also show that the problem is tractable when the number of cuts is small and provide an SMT-based solver to find these optimal configurations. This work helps define the computational limits of circuit decomposition strategies.

Detailed structure

Model / system. The study focuses on the NISQ era quantum circuit model, representing quantum operations as tensors and qubit trajectories as edges within a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) or Tensor Network.

Important parameters / regimes. beta (number of edge duplications/cuts), alpha (number of clusters), k (maximum number of input/output wires per cluster), and the qubit budget Q.

Assumptions / limitations. The analysis focuses on 'timelike' cuts (wire cuts) and assumes 'legal' DAGs where the number of inputs equals the number of outputs.

Figures summary. Figure 1 shows tensor network evolution; Figure 2 illustrates unitary decomposition; Figure 3 shows the graph duplication protocol; Figures 4-7 visualize complexity proofs and SMT solver performance; Figures 8-10 detail the mathematical construction for NP-completeness proofs.

Paper structure. The paper introduces the circuit cutting problem, establishes a graph-theoretic duality, provides a complexity characterization (identifying polynomial and NP-complete regimes), proposes an SMT-based optimization algorithm, and provides rigorous mathematical proofs in the appendix.

Abstract

The current noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) era is characterized by substantial errors and noise, which limit the practical feasibility of deep, many-qubit circuits. To address these constraints, quantum circuit cutting has emerged as a promising tool. Recently, there has been significant research on methods for performing such cutting effectively. In this work, the duality between quantum circuits and classical graphs - specifically, directed acyclic graphs (dags) - is leveraged to analyze the complexity of finding an optimal circuit-cutting configuration that minimizes the number of cuts. After developing a rigorous graph-theoretic framework, the complexity of identifying cut locations that partition a given quantum circuit into smaller fragments is characterized. The corresponding graph-combinatorial task is then defined, and the resulting partition problem is shown to be NP-complete. Furthermore, even a simplified version of the problem, restricted to circuits composed only of one- and two-qubit gates, is shown to be NP-complete. Finally, based on these constraints, an algorithm grounded in satisfiability modulo theories (SMT) is proposed to find optimal cuts when the number of qubits per partition is bounded. This work therefore provides a complexity-theoretic characterization of cut placement and a practical solver for bounded-size decompositions.

Regularization of Divergent Power Sums via Fractional Extension of Differential Generators

Authors: Eric A. Galapon
Type: theory · Category: other · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23544v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. The paper addresses the lack of continuity in standard Riemann zeta regularization when moving from integer to non-integer exponents alpha in divergent power sums.

Main result. The author develops a new regularization prescription using fractional extensions of differential generators that recovers Riemann zeta regularization as a special case while ensuring continuity and providing additional terms determined by the generator.

Method. The method employs fractional calculus, contour integration using Hankel-type contours, and the construction of a Generalized Spectral Function (GSF) via a differential generator L.

Summary. This paper introduces a generalized mathematical framework for regularizing divergent power series. By using fractional extensions of differential operators, the author creates a regularization scheme that is continuous for both integer and non-integer exponents. This method generalizes the well-known Riemann zeta regularization and allows for new regularized values that depend on the specific choice of a differential generator. The work provides a consistent way to handle branch points and singularities in the context of spectral functions.

Detailed structure

Model / system. The mathematical framework is applied to the regularization of divergent sums of the form sum n^alpha, with physical motivation drawn from non-interacting fermions in a box and the calculation of restoring forces.

Key observables. Regularized power sums, regularized products, and the generalized zeta function.

Important parameters / regimes. The exponent alpha (complex order), the differential generator L, and the function h(t) defining the generator.

Assumptions / limitations. The Generalized Spectral Function must have a holomorphic complex extension with a pole at the origin, and the function Phi(z) is assumed to be an entire function.

Figures summary. Figure 1 shows a complex plot of the polylogarithm Li_alpha(exp(-z)) highlighting branch cuts; Figure 2 shows a complex plot of the polylogarithm with a more complex generator, illustrating additional branch cuts.

Paper structure. The paper begins by defining the regularization problem, introduces the differential generator and GSF framework, develops the fractional extension via contour integrals, proves the equivalence of differential and integral forms, and concludes with explicit examples and comparison to Riemann zeta regularization.

Abstract

We reconsider the problem of regularizing the divergent series $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^α$ for $\operatorname{Re}α>-1$, and offer a regularization prescription that yields the Riemann zeta regularization as a special case. The development of the regularization is framed as a two-step problem. The first step is prescribing a regularization of the divergent sum $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^m$ for every non-negative integer $m$; and the second step is the extension of the sum for non-integer $α$. The extension is obtained under the consistency condition that the regularized sum for integer $m$ emerges continuously from the sum for non-integer $α$. The scheme is specified by a differential generator $L=L(\mathrm{d}/\mathrm{d}t)$ through which a generalized spectral function (GSF), $K_L(t)$, is constructed. Under the condition that the GSF has a holomorphic complex extension $K_L(z)$ with $z=0$ as a pole, the case for integer $m$ takes the regularized value $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} n^m = (2πi)^{-1}\oint_C L^m K_L(z) z^{-1}\mathrm{d}z$, where $C$ is a closed contour enclosing only the pole of $K_L(z)$ at the origin. On the other hand, under the consistency condition, the case for non-integer $α$ takes the value $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}n^α=(2πi)^{-1}\int_{\tilde{C}} L^α K_L(z) z^{-1}\mathrm{d}z$, where $L^α$ is the fractional extension of $L^m$ and $\tilde{C}$ is an appropriate deformation of the contour $C$. Here, we obtain the regularization corresponding to the generator $L=h(t) \mathrm{d}/\mathrm{d}t$, where $h(t)$ has the analytic extension $h(z)$ such that $1/h(z)$ is an entire function. We find that the regularized sum is equal to the Riemann zeta regularized value plus terms determined by the generator $L$.

Scissors modes in generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equations

Authors: Neelam Shukla, Oleksandr V. Marchukov, Bastien Humbert, Jan Arlt, Jeremy Armstrong, Artem G. Volosniev
Type: theory · Category: quantum gases · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.23219v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. The study investigates whether the frequency of scissors modes in nonlinear quantum systems depends on the specific form of the nonlinearity (compressibility) in the Thomas-Fermi regime.

Main result. The authors establish that in the Thomas-Fermi regime, the scissors mode frequency is universal and independent of the power-law index of the nonlinearity, acting as a pure shear mode. They also show that the frequency transitions from a non-interacting limit to a universal Thomas-Fermi limit as atom number increases.

Method. The study employs analytical derivation using a linear approximation and the method of characteristics, alongside numerical simulations using the split-step Crank-Nicolably method to solve the generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation.

Summary. This paper investigates the behavior of scissors modes in quantum gases described by generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equations. It proves that in the strongly interacting Thomas-Fermi regime, the frequency of these rotational oscillations is universal and does not depend on the specific type of nonlinearity. By simulating both standard and Lee-Huang-Yang systems, the authors demonstrate how the frequency transitions from the non-interacting to the universal limit as the number of atoms increases. The findings highlight that the scissors mode is a pure shear mode that does not probe system compressibility.

Detailed structure

Model / system. The system consists of degenerate Bose gases, specifically comparing standard Gross-Pitaevskii (GPE) and Lee-Huang-Yang (LHY) regimes, modeled by a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation with arbitrary power-law nonlinearity in an anisotropic harmonic trap.

Key observables. The scissors mode frequency (Omega), the expectation value of the xy coordinate (proxy for oscillation angle), and the angular momentum operator.

Important parameters / regimes. Power-law index (gamma), atom number (N_atoms), interaction strength (g), scattering length (a), and trap frequencies (omega_x, omega_y).

Assumptions / limitations. The analysis assumes the Thomas-Fermi regime (neglecting quantum pressure) and a small rotation angle (theta_0 << 1) for the analytical derivation.

Figures summary. Figure 1 shows scissors mode frequency vs. atom number approaching the TF limit; Figure 2 shows frequency vs. scattering length; Figure 5 illustrates the frequency convergence to the large-atom limit; Figure 6 compares sinusoidal vs. complex multi-mode oscillations at different initial angles.

Paper structure. The paper begins with an analytical derivation of the scissors mode frequency in generalized nonlinear systems, followed by numerical validation using LHY and GPE models, an exploration of the transition between interaction regimes, and an analysis of the effects of large initial perturbations.

Why it may be interesting. This paper is interesting because it identifies a universal, nonlinearity-independent feature (the scissors mode frequency) in quantum fluids, providing a robust diagnostic tool for characterizing different types of quantum liquids like LHY systems.

Abstract

We investigate scissors modes in nonlinear systems with arbitrary power-law dependence of the nonlinear term. Through analytical derivation, we establish a general expression demonstrating that, in the Thomas-Fermi regime, the frequency of the scissors mode is independent of the specific form of the nonlinearity. We conclude that the scissors mode is a shear mode that does not probe the compressibility of the system, which depends on nonlinearity. To validate our findings, we perform numerical simulations of experimentally relevant Lee-Huang-Yang (LHY) systems. Our results illustrate the transition of the scissors mode frequency from the non-interacting to the strongly interacting (Thomas-Fermi) regime. Finally, we demonstrate that the scissors mode frequency remains clearly identifiable even under strong quenches, which should facilitate the experimental observation of our findings.

Semiclassical phases of charged spin-$1/2$ matter-wave interferometers in gravitational wave backgrounds

Authors: Nontapat Wanwieng, Apimook Watcharangkool
Type: theory · Category: other · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.24751v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. Developing a unified, gauge-invariant semiclassical framework to describe how the different components of a charged spin-1/2 matter-wave interferometer phase respond to time-dependent gravitational wave backgrounds.

Main result. The study identifies three distinct, complementary phase channels (dynamical, spin, and electromagnetic AB) that all probe the same tidal curvature scale, with the AB phase specifically arising from GW-induced perturbations to the electromagnetic field.

Method. A semiclassical WKB expansion of the covariant Dirac equation is used within the Fermi Normal Coordinate framework to analyze phase accumulation in a freely falling detector frame.

Summary. This paper provides a unified theoretical description of how charged spin-1/2 matter-wave interferometers sense gravitational waves. It demonstrates that the total interferometric phase consists of three distinct channels: a dynamical phase, a spin-dependent phase, and an electromagnetic Aharonov-Bohm phase. While all three channels are driven by the same tidal curvature scale, they arise from different physical couplings—namely, inertial motion, spin-curvature coupling, and curvature-induced electromagnetic perturbations. This work identifies how spacetime curvature imprints itself on quantum interference through multiple, complementary pathways.

Detailed structure

Model / system. A charged spin-1/2 matter-wave interferometer (such as a Mach-Zehnder configuration) propagating through a weak gravitational wave background with an additional background magnetic field.

Key observables. Total accumulated phase difference, decomposed into dynamical phase, spin holonomy (spin phase), and electromagnetic Aharon-Bohm phase.

Important parameters / regimes. Tidal scale (ddot{h} ~ Omega^2 h0), particle mass (m), charge (q), interferometer side length (L), GW frequency (Omega_gw), and background magnetic field (B0).

Assumptions / limitations. Semiclassical (WKB) limit, long-wavelength limit (L << lambda_gw), weak gravitational wave background, and the quasi-static approximation for tidal fields.

Paper structure. The paper begins with a derivation of the semiclassical framework from the Dirac equation, proceeds to decompose the phase into three physical channels, analyzes the electromagnetic response of the system to curvature, and concludes by comparing the scaling and sensitivity of the different phase components.

Abstract

A matter wave propagating through curved spacetime accumulates phase that encodes both geometry and gauge structure. We develop a semiclassical framework for charged spin-$1/2$ matter-wave interferometers based on a WKB expansion of the covariant Dirac equation, in which the phase decomposes into dynamical, spin, and electromagnetic Aharonov-Bohm (AB) contributions. In a freely falling detector frame, all three channels are governed by local tidal fields. In a weak gravitational-wave (GW) background, the dynamical and spin phases probe the gravitoelectric and gravitomagnetic sectors of curvature, while the AB phase arises from curvature-induced electromagnetic fields obtained from Maxwell's equations in curved spacetime. For a Mach-Zehnder interferometer (MZI), all three responses are determined by the same tidal scale, $\ddot{h}_A \sim Ω^2_{gw}h_0$, and filtered by a common geometric kernel, while entering through distinct physical couplings. In particular, the AB contribution depends not only on the enclosed flux but also on spatial variations of the induced fields and exhibits an intrinsic frequency dependence set by the traversal time. These results provide a unified description of matter-wave interferometric phases in time-dependent GW backgrounds and identify complementary dynamical, spin, and electromagnetic pathways through which spacetime curvature imprints itself on quantum interference.

Solution of a large nonlinear recurrent neural network at fixed connectivity

Authors: Albert J. Wakhloo
Type: theory · Category: disordered systems and neural networks · PDF: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.24141v1
Analysis basis: full PDF text, analyzed in chunks

↔ Scroll figures horizontally

Main problem. Calculating the moments, response functions, and pairwise correlations of a large nonlinear recurrent neural network at a fixed connectivity without averaging over synaptic weights.

Main result. The paper proves a generalized Wick theorem for the network and validates the Shen-Hu conjecture, providing closed-form Fourier space equations for the linear response and correlation functions.

Method. The author uses the Martin-Siggia-Rose-De Dominicis-Janssen (MSRDJ) formalism, a 1/sqrt(N) diagrammatic expansion, and graph theory (ribbon graphs and Euler's theorem) to identify and resum leading-order terms.

Summary. This paper provides an analytical solution for the dynamics of large nonlinear recurrent neural networks at fixed connectivity. By using a diagrammatic expansion, the author shows that the leading-order behavior is dominated by 'chain' diagrams after a specific cancellation of 'rainbow' and 'bubble' diagrams. The work proves the Shen-Hu conjecture and establishes a generalized Wick theorem for these systems.
